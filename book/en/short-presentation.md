# Author Presentation

**Fátima Pérez Robledo** is a multidisciplinary professional combining academic research, teaching, and clinical practice in the field of health and rehabilitation.

She is currently an Assistant Professor in the **Department of Nursing and Physiotherapy** at the **University of Salamanca (USAL)**. She received her PhD from this same university in 2018 with a thesis on the effectiveness of postural control exercise programs in sedentary older adults.

Her research activity focuses primarily on **physiotherapy and neurorehabilitation**. She is a member of prominent research groups such as **GTC Motion-Usal** and **NEUROUSAL**. Her recent works include studies on the use of technologies in rehabilitation (such as the telemetric Fugl-Meyer scale), thermography analysis in stroke patients, and various movement-based therapies (such as dance therapy and Vojta reflex locomotion therapy).

In addition to her academic work in physiotherapy, she has a background as a **music therapist** and **Gestalt therapist**, with experience in accompanying people in different life processes (older adults, dependents, caregivers, and professional musicians). She has collaborated with organizations such as the Alzheimer's Foundation of Spain in the application of music therapy as a therapeutic resource.
