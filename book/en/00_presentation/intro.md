# Author Presentation

In this section, we present the authors of this material.

## Fátima Pérez Robledo

**Fátima Pérez Robledo** is an Assistant Professor in the Department of Nursing and Physiotherapy at the **University of Salamanca (USAL)**.

She received her PhD from the University of Salamanca in 2018 with a thesis on the effectiveness of postural control exercise programs in sedentary older adults. Her research focuses on physiotherapy, particularly in neurorehabilitation, and she is a member of research groups such as **GTC Motion-Usal** and **NEUROUSAL**. Her recent work includes studies on the use of technologies in rehabilitation (such as the telemetric Fugl-Meyer scale), thermography analysis in stroke patients, and movement-based therapies (dance therapy and Vojta reflex locomotion therapy).

## Andrea Calleja Caballero

**Andrea Calleja Caballero** is a Predoctoral Researcher in Training in the **Department of Nursing and Physiotherapy** at the **University of Salamanca**, focusing on the area of Physiotherapy.

She is associated with the **NEUROUSAL** (Neurorehabilitation Research) and **IBSAL** (Institute of Biomedical Research of Salamanca) research groups. Her research lines cover neurorehabilitation, the impact on the mental health of caregivers (e.g., for people with dementia or cerebral palsy), and physiotherapy in patients with acquired brain damage. She has participated as a principal investigator in studies on the benefits of strength training in informal caregivers of patients with Alzheimer's disease.
